Descending into form

To become flesh and blood, spirits leave the pure heavens and encapsulate themselves into a bodily form, constructing a shell of matter around themselves as they grow. We are a mix of consciousness (Spirit) and unconsciousness (Matter).

Since our departure from Eden, we have lost the clarity that Adam and Eve had within their souls - they were great souls - containing within them the potential of all of the souls of humanity. With each division by reproduction, and through the toil of life, we struggled to remain connected to the original pure understanding of what it meant to be a human being. We are now lost souls desperately in need of redemption - and indeed we have been redeemed by the act of Jesus Christ to overcome death and bring us into the family of Spiritual Israel.

The original Godliness of Adam and Eve’s souls has not been lost, however. Only fragmented among the billions of human beings alive today. Zion is both the slow and steady process of recognizing and ordering these soul components to rectify a fallen world, and the name of the destination we seek: A new Heaven and a new Earth.

Isaiah foresaw this process in action:

Isaiah 40:4 (NKJV)

Every valley shall be exalted
And every mountain and hill brought low;
The crooked places shall be made straight
And the rough places smooth

God didn’t just create an end state. He created a journey, to refine us according to his will for our character. We know we were made for this purpose - even the “evil” Pharaoh was raised up for God’s will to be done:

Romans 9:17 (NKJV)

For the Scripture says to the Pharaoh,
“For this very purpose I have raised you up, that I may show My power in you, and that My name may be declared in all the earth.”

Zion - a state of universal perfection

When the awareness of God’s reality dawns again on humankind, evil will be routed in an apocalyptic battle. The truth will be revealed, and God’s presence will once again be tangible in Jerusalem:

Zechariah 8:1-3 (NKJV)

Again the word of the Lord of hosts came, saying,
2 “Thus says the Lord of hosts:
‘I am zealous for Zion with great zeal;
With great fervor I am zealous for her.’

3 “Thus says the Lord:
‘I will return to Zion,
And dwell in the midst of Jerusalem.
Jerusalem shall be called the City of Truth,
The Mountain of the Lord of hosts,
The Holy Mountain.’

How do we prepare for such a reality? Knowing it is our destiny, we must continue to exhort one another towards goodness and truth. There is no area of our lives that may be safely ignored - we must be all-inclusive. The clock is ticking, there is no time to waste in preparing for the final Revelation.

Dr. Deanna Minich has developed a comprehensive map of our reality, which outlines every possible arena for refinement. As we prepare for Zion, let’s take a close look at each of these areas in our lives, and do our very best as a community of faith to enhance the Temple of God’s Holy Spirit, our bodies. This is what it means to present ourselves a “living sacrifice” to the Lord:


Romans 12:1 (NKJV)

I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, which is your reasonable service.

1 Corinthians 6:19-20 (NKJV)

Or do you not know that your body is the temple of the Holy Spirit who is in you, whom you have from God, and you are not your own? 20 For you were bought at a price; therefore glorify God in your body and in your spirit, which are God’s.
